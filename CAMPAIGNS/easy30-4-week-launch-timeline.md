# Easy30 4-Week Launch Timeline

Last updated: 2026-05-29

## Purpose

Use this as the organized zero-budget launch checklist for ReadEasy30 and MathEasy30.

The goal is steady execution without burnout.

## Launch rule

Do the work in order.

Do not jump to affiliate links, outreach, or live forms until the sites have the proper pages, disclosures, and reviewed language.

## Week 1 — Site preparation

### ReadEasy30

- Review current `app.html`, `app.js`, and `css/style.css` before adding any timer code.
- Add the Bubbles 30-minute timer only after checking the current lesson flow.
- Add the ReadEasy30 success card that optionally links to MathEasy30.
- Add or verify Privacy Policy page.
- Add or verify Monetization / Affiliate Disclosure page.
- Verify language does not make medical, diagnosis, or guaranteed-result claims.

### MathEasy30

- Review current math app files before adding any timer code.
- Add the MathEasy30 30-minute timer only after checking the current lesson flow.
- Add the MathEasy30 success card that optionally links to ReadEasy30.
- Add or verify Privacy Policy page.
- Add or verify Monetization / Affiliate Disclosure page.
- Verify language does not make medical, diagnosis, or guaranteed-result claims.

### Cloudflare / performance

- Confirm both sites publish from GitHub.
- Confirm mobile pages load quickly.
- Confirm no broken navigation links.
- Confirm caching is working safely.
- Do not add live tracking scripts without approval.

## Week 2 — Printables and Pinterest

### ReadEasy30 printables

- Publish one clean, distraction-free printable reading worksheet page.
- Use large font and generous line spacing.
- Add a printable-friendly layout.
- Include Bubbles tip box.
- Add practical reading content such as signs, notes, schedules, or forms.

### Monetization planning

- Keep affiliate placement planned only until approval.
- Do not add live affiliate links until disclosure language and product selection are reviewed.
- When approved, place affiliate/resource link at the bottom of worksheet pages, not above the lesson.

### Pinterest

- Create a Pinterest Business account for ReadEasy30.
- Link it to `https://readeasy30.com`.
- Create 3 vertical pins, 1000 x 1500 pixels.
- Use calm colors and Bubbles-friendly style.
- Point pins to the printable page when live.
- Track pins in the posting tracker.

## Week 3 — Grassroots community outreach

### Reddit

- Find 3 communities where helpful education resources are allowed.
- Read rules before posting.
- Do not spam.
- Do not use medical claims.
- Use feedback-seeking language.

Candidate communities to research:

- parent communities
- adult literacy communities
- homeschool communities
- ESL communities
- math help communities

### Facebook

- Join 5 targeted Facebook groups.
- Read each group’s rules.
- Do not post if links or self-promotion are banned.
- Use the Facebook homeschool outreach blueprint.
- Lead with free resource value and printables.
- Track responses and questions.

## Week 4 — Professional outreach

### Library and literacy outreach

- Build a list of 15 local public library branches, literacy nonprofits, adult education groups, tutoring programs, or community learning organizations.
- Find the best contact: Youth Services Librarian, Adult Services Librarian, Literacy Coordinator, Program Director, or general resource email.
- Send a simple personalized email.
- Offer the sites as free learning resources.
- Do not exaggerate claims.
- Track replies.

## Weekly scoreboard

| Week | Main goal | Success measure | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Site prep | Timer/cross-promo specs reviewed and pages ready | Pending |
| Week 2 | Printables/Pinterest | One worksheet page and 3 pins ready | Pending |
| Week 3 | Community outreach | 3 Reddit/community posts or comments and 5 FB groups reviewed | Pending |
| Week 4 | Professional outreach | 15 library/nonprofit contacts collected and emailed | Pending |

## Blocked until review

- live affiliate links
- live tracking scripts
- automated posting
- mass email automation
- claims about ADHD, dyslexia, autism, dyscalculia, or special education outcomes
- claims about zero tracking unless the live site is verified
- claims about official K-8 coverage unless the curriculum path is complete and reviewed
