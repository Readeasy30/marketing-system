# Education Facebook Active Plan

Purpose: use Facebook for trust-building posts, parent/helper conversations, adult learner support, and careful community sharing where allowed.

## Facebook job

Facebook should build trust and discussion. It should not feel like spam.

Best uses:

- personal/page posts
- parent/helper posts
- adult learner encouragement
- tutoring/literacy resource sharing
- group posts only where allowed
- comments that answer questions helpfully

## Core rules

- Do not spam groups.
- Read group rules before posting.
- Do not post links in groups that do not allow links.
- If unsure, post a helpful no-link discussion first.
- Use no-shame language.
- Do not claim guaranteed improvement.
- Do not mention diagnosis or special education outcomes as a promise.

## Post templates

### Parent/helper reading post

Helping a reader does not have to feel stressful.

ReadEasy30 gives short lessons, read aloud support, simple questions, and calm practice without pressure.

Start with one short lesson and let the reader go slowly.

Link: https://readeasy30.com/app.html

### Reading worksheet post

Need reading practice away from the screen?

ReadEasy30 has free printable reading worksheets with short passages, vocabulary help, questions, and helper tips.

Good for parents, tutors, adult learners, and ESL learners.

Link: https://readeasy30.com/printable-reading-worksheets.html

### Adult reading post

Adults can rebuild reading confidence privately.

ReadEasy30 is simple, respectful, and calm. No public score. No shame. Just short reading practice one step at a time.

Link: https://readeasy30.com/adult-reading-practice-without-shame.html

### Math post

Math practice should not feel like a panic test.

MathEasy30 helps learners practice one small skill at a time with hints, drawings, and calm coaching.

Confidence first. Speed later.

Link: https://matheasy30.com/app.html

### Group-safe no-link discussion

What makes reading or math practice feel stressful for learners?

For many people, it is not laziness. It is speed, embarrassment, confusing directions, or not knowing where to start.

What kind of simple practice would help most?

## Comment response bank

### If someone asks if it is free

Yes. The current practice pages are free to use. Some progress may stay on the device through browser storage, but no account is required for the current version.

### If someone asks if it replaces tutoring

No. It is practice support, not a replacement for a teacher, tutor, school plan, or specialist. It is meant to help learners practice calmly.

### If someone says adult reading is embarrassing

That is exactly why the language is simple and respectful. The goal is private practice without shame.

### If someone asks where to start

Start with the ReadEasy30 app for reading or MathEasy30 app for math. If paper practice is easier, use the worksheet hubs first.

## Review after 14 days

- Which post got comments?
- Which post got link clicks if known?
- Did parent, adult learner, ESL, worksheet, or math content work best?
- Did no-link discussion posts produce useful questions?
- Which objections need a new FAQ or video?

## Decision rule

If discussion posts get better response than link posts, use discussion first and share links only when asked or where allowed.
