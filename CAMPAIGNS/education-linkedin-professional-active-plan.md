# Education LinkedIn / Professional Active Plan

Purpose: use LinkedIn and professional-style posts to reach tutors, literacy volunteers, librarians, nonprofit workers, ESL helpers, workforce-development people, and community education contacts.

## LinkedIn job

LinkedIn should build credibility and resource awareness. It should not feel like a hard sell.

Best uses:

- Share the mission behind ReadEasy30 and MathEasy30.
- Explain the no-shame education approach.
- Reach tutors, libraries, nonprofits, ESL groups, and community educators.
- Ask for feedback from helpers and professionals.
- Document what is being built.

## Core rules

- Keep the tone practical and respectful.
- Do not claim guaranteed improvement.
- Do not claim medical, diagnosis, or official school outcomes.
- Do not overstate AI.
- Lead with usefulness and access.
- Invite feedback.

## Post templates

### Mission post

Reading practice should feel calm, not embarrassing.

That is the idea behind ReadEasy30: short passages, read-aloud support, vocabulary help, simple questions, and no-shame daily practice.

It is not a replacement for a teacher or tutor. It is a simple free practice tool for learners who need a slower starting point.

Resource: https://readeasy30.com/app.html

Feedback welcome from tutors, literacy volunteers, ESL helpers, and adult education folks.

### Worksheet resource post

I am building free printable reading practice resources for learners who need short, calm practice.

The goal is simple:

- short passages
- useful vocabulary
- simple questions
- helper tips
- respectful wording for adults and older learners

Worksheet hub: https://readeasy30.com/printable-reading-worksheets.html

I would welcome feedback from tutors, librarians, literacy volunteers, and ESL helpers.

### Adult literacy post

Adult reading practice needs to be respectful.

Many adults want private, simple reading practice without embarrassment. ReadEasy30 is being built around that idea: short lessons, read aloud support, vocabulary help, and no public scoring.

Adult reading page: https://readeasy30.com/adult-reading-practice-without-shame.html

What would make a resource like this more useful for adult learners?

### Math post

Math practice should not feel like a panic test.

MathEasy30 is the sister site to ReadEasy30. It focuses on one small math skill at a time: counting, addition, subtraction, word problems, math facts, and fractions.

The approach is confidence first, speed later.

Start page: https://matheasy30.com/app.html

### Professional feedback post

Question for tutors, librarians, ESL helpers, and literacy volunteers:

When someone needs beginner reading or math practice, what is usually missing from free resources?

Is it printable practice, adult-friendly wording, read-aloud support, simple instructions, or something else?

I am using the answers to improve ReadEasy30 and MathEasy30.

## Comment response bank

### If someone offers feedback

Thank you. That is exactly the kind of practical feedback I need. I am trying to keep the resources simple, respectful, and useful for real practice.

### If someone asks if it is free

Yes, the current public practice pages are free. No account is required for the current version.

### If someone asks about AI

The goal is not to make the site feel complicated. AI may help generate or organize future practice materials, but the learner experience should stay simple and calm.

### If someone asks if it replaces a teacher

No. It is practice support, not a replacement for a teacher, tutor, school plan, specialist, or professional assessment.
