# Parent Helper Week Campaign

Last updated: 2026-05-28

## Campaign Goal

Help parents, tutors, grandparents, homeschool helpers, churches, libraries, and community groups support learners with calm reading and math practice.

## Campaign Name

Help Without Pressure

## Simple Promise

You do not need to be a perfect teacher. A calm daily routine helps.

## Audience

- Parents helping children at home
- Grandparents helping with homework
- Tutors and homeschool helpers
- Church and library volunteers
- Adults helping ESL learners

## Core Message

A learner who struggles may need less pressure and more structure.

ReadEasy30 gives a calm reading path.
MathEasy30 gives a calm math path.

Helpers can sit beside the learner, go slowly, ask one question at a time, and praise effort.

## Social Post Drafts

### Post 1

A calm helper can change the whole lesson.

Sit beside the learner.
Go slowly.
Ask one question at a time.
Praise effort.

Small practice counts.

### Post 2

Try this when a learner freezes:

1. Pause.
2. Read the question again.
3. Cover extra information.
4. Ask, "What is one step we can try?"

Less pressure. More progress.

### Post 3

Parents do not need to become teachers overnight.

Start with one short reading task or one math task.
Keep it calm.
Come back tomorrow.

### Post 4

A simple home routine:

10 minutes reading.
10 minutes math.
10 minutes review.

The goal is not perfect. The goal is steady.

### Post 5

Struggling learners often hear what they did wrong.

Try saying:

"Good effort. Let's look again."

That one sentence keeps the door open.

## Short Video Scripts

### Script 1: Homework fight reset

Hook: If homework turns into a fight, stop and reset.

Say: Lower the pressure. Pick one small task. Read slowly. Ask one question. Let the learner think.

Close: Calm practice beats rushed practice.

### Script 2: Parent helper sentence

Hook: Here is one sentence that helps struggling learners.

Say: Say, "Good effort. Let's look again." It corrects without shame and keeps the learner trying.

Close: Small words matter.

### Script 3: 30-minute routine

Hook: Try this simple 30-minute learning routine.

Say: Ten minutes reading. Ten minutes math. Ten minutes review. Keep the work short and calm.

Close: Repeat tomorrow.
