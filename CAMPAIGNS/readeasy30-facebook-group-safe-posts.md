## Post 4 — Helpful tip without link

A simple reading helper tip:

After a short story, ask only one question at a time.

Start with:

1. Who was in the story?
2. What happened?
3. Where did it happen?

If the reader struggles, read the story together once. Then try again calmly.

## Comment replies

### If someone asks if it is free

Right now the goal is to make the practice easy to start. It is built as a simple online reading-practice resource.

### If someone asks what age it is for

It is not meant as an official grade-placement tool. It is for simple reading practice with parent/helper support, ESL learners, adult learners, and anyone who wants a calmer path.

### If someone says their child hates reading

That is common. I would keep practice short, read together first, praise effort, and stop before frustration takes over. Pressure usually makes reading harder.

### If someone asks whether it replaces tutoring

No. It is a practice resource. A tutor, teacher, or literacy specialist can still be very important, especially when a learner needs deeper support.
