# PetNeeds.ai Marketing Plan

Last updated: 2026-06-02

## Goal

Build PetNeeds.ai into a trusted, simple pet-care help site that earns traffic first, then monetizes through safe affiliate links, local sponsor placements, display ads, email list growth, and downloadable checklists.

Website code stays in the PetNeeds.ai website repo. This file controls the marketing system.

## Core Positioning

**PetNeeds.ai helps everyday pet owners know what to do, what to watch for, and when to call a veterinarian.**

Keep the language simple enough for busy families. No fear tricks. No fake vet claims. No diagnosis.

## Main Audience Groups

| Audience | What they need | Best content angle | Revenue path later |
|---|---|---|---|
| New dog owners | simple puppy steps | new puppy checklist, house safety, vet visit prep | puppy supplies, insurance education, local vets/groomers |
| New cat owners | kitten and litter-box basics | new kitten checklist, litter-box setup, scratching basics | cat supplies, litter products, local vets/sitters |
| Families with kids | safe pet habits | child + pet safety, routines, warning signs | printables, local trainers, family pet products |
| Beginner fish owners | avoid bowl/tank mistakes | goldfish, tank basics, water care | aquarium supplies, local pet stores |
| Turtle/reptile questions | what to do safely | found turtle outside, beginner habitat basics | reptile supplies, wildlife/resource pages |
| Worried pet owners | when to call a vet | symptom warning signs, poison safety, emergency checklist | trust first, later vet directories/sponsors |

## Brand Voice

Use this tone everywhere:

- warm
- calm
- practical
- family-friendly
- safety-first
- plain English
- helpful without pretending to be a veterinarian

Avoid:

- panic headlines
- diagnosis language
- medication dosing
- promises that a product solves health problems
- guilt language
- burying affiliate/sponsor disclosures

## Traffic Strategy

### 1. SEO First

PetNeeds.ai should win with helpful long-tail pages, not generic pet blog posts.

Best first SEO topics:

1. New Puppy Checklist
2. New Kitten Checklist
3. Signs Your Pet Needs a Vet
4. Goldfish Care for Beginners
5. Turtle Found Outside: What To Do
6. Beginner Fish Tank Mistakes
7. Dog Safety Around Children
8. Cat Litter Box Basics
9. Pet Supplies Checklist for New Owners
10. What To Ask Before Choosing a Veterinarian

Every article should include:

- quick answer near the top
- safe steps
- warning signs
- veterinarian reminder
- related internal links
- simple FAQ section
- clear date reviewed/updated

### 2. Pinterest

Pinterest fits PetNeeds.ai because pet owners search for checklists, safety tips, and beginner guides.

Use vertical pins for:

- new puppy checklist
- new kitten checklist
- goldfish beginner mistakes
- turtle found outside
- signs your pet needs a vet
- family pet safety rules

Pin format:

- title: simple problem phrase
- image: pet + checklist or safe home scene
- description: 1 helpful sentence + PetNeeds.ai
- destination: matching guide page

### 3. YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Instagram Reels

Use simple 20-40 second videos. No complex editing at first.

Video formula:

1. Hook: “Bringing home a puppy? Check these 5 things first.”
2. 3-5 practical tips.
3. Safety reminder.
4. CTA: “Get the checklist at PetNeeds.ai.”

### 4. Facebook

Use Facebook for family-friendly posts and local sharing later.

Best Facebook content:

- checklist graphics
- local pet safety reminders
- “ask before choosing a vet” posts
- seasonal pet safety reminders
- shareable family pet rules

### 5. Local Sponsor Growth Later

After trust pages exist, prepare city/category sponsor pages.

Potential sponsor categories:

- veterinarians
- groomers
- trainers
- pet sitters
- dog walkers
- pet stores
- aquarium shops
- shelters/rescues

Do not add paid local listings until disclosure copy and review standards are ready.

## Trust Rules

PetNeeds.ai should show trust before monetization.

Required trust pieces:

- clear About page
- veterinary safety disclaimer
- affiliate disclosure before affiliate links
- local sponsor disclosure before paid placements
- article review/update dates
- author/editor note
- source links for health/safety pages
- no AI-only medical claims

## Monetization Ladder

### Stage 1: Trust + Traffic

- publish core guides
- create social graphics
- start Pinterest pins
- build email checklist offer
- collect no payments on site

### Stage 2: Soft Monetization

- affiliate links only on supply/checklist pages
- no affiliate links on urgent health pages unless extremely careful
- use clear disclosures near links
- recommend categories first, products second

### Stage 3: Local Sponsors

- sponsor inquiry page
- local listing standards
- paid placement disclosure
- city/category pages

### Stage 4: Display Ads

Only after traffic is meaningful and UX is stable. Ads should not block emergency or vet-safety content.

## First Offer / Lead Magnet

Create a free downloadable checklist:

**“New Pet Starter Checklist: Food, Safety, Vet Visit, Daily Routine”**

Use it to collect email signups later.

## First 30-Day Success Targets

- 10 core articles drafted
- 20 social posts prepared
- 15 Canva prompts prepared
- 10 Pinterest pin ideas prepared
- 1 lead magnet planned
- affiliate disclosure drafted
- local sponsor plan drafted
- no unsafe health claims

## Source-Based Rules

Use these current policy rules when creating PetNeeds.ai content:

- Google rewards original, helpful, people-first content that shows experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust. Do not use AI to mass-produce low-value pages for ranking manipulation.
- FTC-style affiliate/sponsor disclosures must be clear, easy to notice, and close to the recommendation.
- Poison or emergency pages must tell users to contact a veterinarian or poison-control resource right away when exposure is suspected.
