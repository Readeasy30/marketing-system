# New Pet Starter Checklist

Bringing home a new pet is exciting.

It is also easier when you start with the basics.

Use this checklist to prepare your home, supplies, daily routine, and first vet questions.

> **Pet safety note:** This checklist gives general pet-care information. It does not replace a veterinarian or qualified pet professional.

## 1. Safe Space

Prepare a calm place where your pet can rest.

Check off:

- [ ] quiet sleeping or habitat area
- [ ] safe food and water spot
- [ ] no loose cords nearby
- [ ] no medicine or cleaners within reach
- [ ] no small items that can be swallowed
- [ ] safe door, window, cage, tank, or enclosure setup
- [ ] pet can leave busy activity and rest

## 2. Food And Water

Check off:

- [ ] food made for the pet's species and life stage
- [ ] clean water setup
- [ ] food and water bowls or proper feeder
- [ ] feeding instructions saved
- [ ] no sudden food changes unless advised by a vet
- [ ] safe storage for food

## 3. Basic Supplies

Most pets need simple, safe supplies first.

Check what applies:

- [ ] bed, crate, cage, tank, or habitat
- [ ] carrier or transport plan
- [ ] cleaning supplies
- [ ] safe toys or enrichment
- [ ] brush or grooming tool if needed
- [ ] ID tag or microchip discussion if appropriate
- [ ] litter box or waste cleanup plan if needed
- [ ] aquarium or habitat equipment if needed

## 4. First Vet Or Professional Care Plan

Check off:

- [ ] veterinarian contact saved
- [ ] emergency clinic contact saved
- [ ] first visit scheduled if needed
- [ ] records gathered
- [ ] food questions written down
- [ ] vaccine or preventive-care questions written down
- [ ] behavior or bathroom questions written down

## 5. Family Routine

Decide before the pet comes home:

- [ ] who feeds the pet
- [ ] who cleans bowls, litter, tank, cage, or habitat
- [ ] where the pet rests
- [ ] what rooms or areas are off limits
- [ ] how children should interact with the pet
- [ ] who watches for health or behavior changes
- [ ] who handles vet calls

## 6. First Night Setup

For the first night, prepare:

- [ ] quiet room or safe area
- [ ] water access
- [ ] clean bed or habitat
- [ ] food plan
- [ ] cleanup supplies nearby
- [ ] calm voices and short visits
- [ ] no forced handling or loud play

## 7. Warning Signs To Watch For

Contact a veterinarian or qualified professional if your pet has:

- [ ] trouble breathing
- [ ] repeated vomiting
- [ ] repeated diarrhea
- [ ] sudden weakness
- [ ] heavy bleeding
- [ ] signs of strong pain
- [ ] refusal to eat with tiredness or weakness
- [ ] bathroom trouble
- [ ] symptoms that are getting worse
- [ ] possible unsafe-food, medicine, plant, or chemical exposure
