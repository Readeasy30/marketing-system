# Pet Supplies Checklist for New Owners

New pet supplies can get expensive fast.

Start with the basics first. Then add extras after you understand your pet’s real needs.

> **Pet safety note:** This page gives general pet-care information. It does not replace a veterinarian or qualified pet professional.

## Quick Answer

Most new pets need safe food and water access, a clean place to sleep or live, cleaning supplies, safe toys or enrichment, identification when appropriate, and a plan for veterinary care.

The exact supplies depend on the pet type.

## Basic Supplies For Most New Pets

Start with:

- food made for the pet’s species and life stage
- clean water setup
- safe bed, crate, cage, tank, or habitat
- cleaning supplies
- safe toys or enrichment
- carrier or transport plan
- vet contact information
- emergency contact information
- records folder

## Dog Supplies

For a new dog or puppy, consider:

- dog or puppy food
- food and water bowls
- collar or harness
- leash
- ID tag
- crate, pen, or safe sleeping space
- washable bedding
- poop bags
- chew toys
- grooming brush
- pet-safe cleaner

For puppies, also plan for frequent potty breaks and chew-safe spaces.

## Cat Supplies

For a new cat or kitten, consider:

- cat or kitten food
- food and water bowls
- litter box
- litter scoop
- litter
- carrier
- scratching post or pad
- bed or blanket
- safe toys
- brush
- pet-safe cleaner

Place the litter box in a quiet, easy-to-reach spot.

## Fish Supplies

For beginner fish, supplies may include:

- properly sized aquarium
- filter
- heater if the fish species needs one
- water conditioner
- water test kit
- safe fish food
- net
- aquarium-safe decorations or hiding spots
- cleaning tools

Do not assume a small bowl is enough. Fish need stable water conditions and the right setup for their species.

## Turtle Or Reptile Supplies

Reptiles and turtles often need very specific care.

Supplies may include:

- proper enclosure
- safe heat source
- UVB lighting if needed by the species
- thermometer
- humidity gauge if needed
- proper food
- water area or dish depending on species
- safe substrate
- hiding area

Do not take a wild turtle home as a pet. If you found a turtle outside, follow local wildlife guidance.

## Supplies To Avoid Buying Too Fast

Wait before buying:

- large bundles of random toys
- expensive gadgets
- trendy products with big promises
- supplements without veterinary guidance
- complicated training tools
- food that does not match the pet’s age or species

Simple and safe is better than fancy and confusing.

## First Vet Visit Folder

Keep a folder with:

- adoption, breeder, shelter, or rescue paperwork
- vaccine records
- medicine notes if any
- feeding notes
- questions for the vet
- emergency clinic contact

## Helpful Buying Rule

Before buying anything, ask:

1. Does my pet truly need this?
2. Is it safe for this pet’s size, age, and species?
3. Can I clean it easily?
4. Does it solve a real problem?
5. Would my veterinarian or qualified pet professional have concerns?

## Common Questions

### What should I buy first for a new pet?

Buy food, water setup, safe housing or sleeping space, cleaning supplies, and transport basics first.

### Should I buy everything before the pet comes home?

Buy the basics first. Add extras after you learn your pet’s size, behavior, health, and routine.

### Are cheap pet supplies okay?

Sometimes. The important question is whether the item is safe, washable, the right size, and useful.

### Should I buy supplements for a new pet?

Ask your veterinarian first. Do not start supplements just because an ad says they are needed.

## Final Reminder

A good starter setup does not have to be fancy.

Focus on safety, daily care, cleanliness, and your first vet visit.
