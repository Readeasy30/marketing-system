# ReadEasy30 + MathEasy30 Education Growth System

Last updated: 2026-06-02

This folder is the working marketing command center for ReadEasy30 and MathEasy30.

The goal is simple: bring steady, safe traffic to both free education websites without adding live ads, tracking, payments, accounts, public AI, or risky code yet.

## Sites covered

- ReadEasy30: calm reading practice, printable reading worksheets, adult reading support, ESL reading support, parent/tutor support.
- MathEasy30: calm math practice, printable math worksheets, addition, word problems, math facts, parent/tutor support.

## Main audience promise

Free, calm, no-shame practice for people who want better reading or math skills in small daily steps.

## Primary audiences

1. Adults who need reading or math practice but do not want to feel embarrassed.
2. ESL learners who need simple English reading and real-life practice.
3. Parents helping children who are behind in reading or math.
4. Tutors, adult education volunteers, libraries, churches, nonprofits, and community programs.
5. Older learners who want patient, private practice.
6. Special education and extra-support learners who need simple repetition and emotional safety.

## Marketing rules

- Use plain language.
- Do not shame learners.
- Do not promise diagnosis, cure, grade improvement, or guaranteed results.
- Do not mass-send outreach.
- Use public official contact forms and public organization contact routes only.
- Keep emails and messages one-to-one, short, and useful.
- Offer free resources first.
- Build trust before monetization.
- Add no live ads, tracking, affiliate links, payments, accounts, upload systems, public AI, private keys, or API keys without direct approval.

## Current growth system parts

1. Audience map.
2. SEO and content clusters.
3. 30-day launch calendar.
4. Outreach copy and scripts.
5. Social post library.
6. Future lead magnet and email plan.
7. Manual QA and blocker log.

## Best first traffic path

1. Strengthen worksheet hubs.
2. Publish simple SEO support pages.
3. Create Pinterest pins for worksheets.
4. Create YouTube Shorts from existing worksheet tips.
5. Use safe outreach to libraries, literacy councils, adult education groups, homeschool groups, tutoring centers, and community nonprofits.
6. Cross-link ReadEasy30 and MathEasy30 where helpful.

## Fast daily workflow

Do this each working day:

1. Pick one audience.
2. Pick one helpful resource.
3. Create one short post or outreach message.
4. Record where it went.
5. Add one improvement idea to the next queue.
6. Stop before doing spammy volume.
