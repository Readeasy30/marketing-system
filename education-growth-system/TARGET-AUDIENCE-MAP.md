# Target Audience Map: ReadEasy30 + MathEasy30

Last updated: 2026-06-02

## Core positioning

ReadEasy30 and MathEasy30 are free, calm, no-shame practice websites for reading and math.

They are built for people who need simple daily practice without feeling talked down to.

## Audience 1: Adults who struggle with reading or math

### What they want

- Private practice.
- Simple lessons.
- No embarrassment.
- Help with everyday reading and math.
- A way to improve without going back to school right away.

### Message

Practice reading and math privately, one small step at a time.

### Best pages to promote

- ReadEasy30 adult reading pages.
- Functional literacy worksheets.
- MathEasy30 math facts and word problem pages.

### Best channels

- Libraries.
- Adult education centers.
- Literacy councils.
- Workforce readiness groups.
- Community Facebook groups, where allowed.

### Content angles

- Reading signs, forms, schedules, and labels.
- Simple math for shopping, time, bills, and work.
- 30 minutes a day, no shame.

## Audience 2: ESL learners

### What they want

- Simple English practice.

- Reading confidence.

Practice simple English reading and everyday words in calm daily lessons.

### Best pages to promote

- ESL reading practice.

- Math word problem practice with simple language.

### Best channels

- Libraries.
- ESL tutors.

### Content angles

## Audience 3: Parents helping children who are behind

### What they want

- A simple plan.
- Non-scary practice.
- Something free to start today.
- Printable worksheets.
- Less arguing at homework time.

### Message

A calm 30-day practice helper for reading and math at home.

### Best pages to promote

- Parent/tutor guide.
- Printable reading worksheets.
- Printable math worksheets.
- Lesson app pages.

### Best channels

- Parent Facebook groups.
- Homeschool communities.
- PTA/community boards.
- Tutors.
- School-adjacent resource pages where submissions are allowed.

### Content angles

- Try 10 calm minutes first.
- Use one worksheet and stop before frustration.
- Praise effort, not speed.
- Make practice feel safe.

## Priority order

Start with these because they are the cleanest and safest:

1. Libraries and adult education programs.
2. Adult learners and ESL learners.
3. Parents and tutors.

## Do not target yet

Avoid paid ads, school district sales, medical/diagnostic claims, and child-data collection until legal, privacy, and trust systems are stronger.
